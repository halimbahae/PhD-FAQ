Frequently Asked Questions

General Re-enrollment & Derogation

Q1: What is the re-enrollment process for the 2025-2026 academic year?

+

A1: PhD students regularly enrolled for the 2024-2025 academic year can submit their re-enrollment request online. Your progress reports must be validated by your thesis supervisor and the CEDOC director for re-enrollment authorization.

Q2: Are 3rd, 4th, and 5th-year students required to submit a derogation request for re-enrollment?

+

A2: Yes, students in their 3rd, 4th, and 5th years must submit a derogation request in addition to their re-enrollment application. This request needs to be approved by your thesis supervisor, the CEDOC director, and the head of the doctoral training establishment.

Q3: Where can I find the conditions for derogation?

+

A3: The conditions for derogation are specified in the Thesis Charter validated by the university council in 2021.

Q4: Is there a specific template for the progress report that needs to be submitted to CEDOC?

+

A4: As of now, a specific template for the progress report has not been shared. Please inquire with CEDOC for the required elements to include.

Q5: Will there be a paper dossier to submit after online re-enrollment?

+

A5: It is believed that a paper dossier, including photocopies of attestations, certificates, and re-enrollment receipts, will still be required, similar to previous years. Further details will be communicated.

Training & Required Units

Q6: What are the required training units for PhD students now, and is the 200-hour requirement still applicable?

+

A6: The focus has shifted from a fixed number of hours (like 200 hours) to the validation of training units. You need to attend conferences and training sessions and obtain certificates in the following areas:

  • Research Methodology
  • History of Sciences
  • Artificial Intelligence
  • Training related to your thesis domain
  • Training outside your thesis domain
  • Intellectual Property
  • Entrepreneurship
  • Pedagogical Innovation
  • Rosetta Stone (language training)

Q7: What kind of formations count towards the required units?

+

A7: Formations already undertaken during Doctoriales and other conferences count towards these units.

Q8: Can online courses from platforms like Coursera, Udemy, or LinkedIn Learning be counted towards the required training units?

+

A8: This information is currently being verified. Please stay tuned for an official clarification on which MOOCs are accepted.

Q9: Where can I access the Moodle E-learning LMS platform for formations?

+

A9: The Moodle platform is currently being set up, and formations will be added progressively.

Q10: Are the required formations available remotely or in person?

+

A10: Both distance and in-person formations are acceptable, as long as you obtain a certificate of participation.

Rosetta Stone Language Platform

Q11: Who can access the Rosetta Stone platform?

+

A11: The Rosetta Stone platform is currently available only for 1st and 2nd-year PhD students.

Q12: What is the required volume of hours for Rosetta Stone?

+

A12: The required volume is 30 hours per year in total, covering both French and English. It is not 30 hours per language. This information is currently being re-verified for final confirmation.

Q13: Why might my Rosetta Stone account be deactivated or inaccessible?

+

A13: Your account may be deactivated automatically at the end of the academic year. If you encounter issues, please contact Mr. Zine El Abidine Triqui at z.triqui@um5r.ac.ma.

Q14: How can I access the Rosetta Stone platform?

+

A14: You can access Rosetta Stone via Moodle:

  1. Log in to Moodle with your UM5 credentials.
  2. Go to this link: https://moodle-fsr.um5.ac.ma/course/index.php?categoryid=4078
  3. Follow the instructions to register or activate your access.
